Security Reviews & Vulnerability Assessment

Understanding the risk
before it understands you.

A residential security assessment or vulnerability review is the starting point for any meaningful security decision. Fortis provides structured, evidence-based security reviews for private clients, estates, and corporate premises across the UK - written to a standard that can be relied upon by professionals and decision-makers.

What a security review covers

More than a walk
around the property.


Our approach

Anyone can walk around a house and grade the locks. A Fortis security review begins with the client's circumstances, their threat picture, and the realistic risks they face. The property is assessed in that context, not in isolation.

  • 01
    Threat identification

    We establish the realistic threat picture for the client - who or what presents risk, what form that risk may take, and what the probability of materialisation is given their specific circumstances and profile.

  • 02
    Physical environment assessment

    A structured inspection of the property, its perimeter, access points, surveillance coverage, lighting, and physical security measures. Assessed against the threat picture established in stage one, not against a generic checklist.

  • 03
    Operational and behavioural review

    Assessment of routines, movement patterns, digital exposure, staff vetting practices, and any operational habits that may create exploitable vulnerability. Security is not just about buildings.

  • 04
    Written report and recommendations

    Findings are delivered as a structured written report with prioritised, actionable recommendations. The report is written to a standard appropriate for use by insurers, legal advisors, estate managers, and family office professionals.

The report standard

Written to be relied
upon, not filed away.


Many security reviews produce a document that tells the client what they already knew. Fortis reports are written differently. Every finding is referenced to the specific threat context that makes it relevant. Every recommendation is costed in operational and practical terms, not just described.

The report is produced to a standard appropriate for reliance by professional third parties. Insurers, legal advisors, estate managers, and family offices regularly require security documentation of this kind. We write to that standard as a matter of course, not as an optional upgrade.

Reports are delivered in confidence and handled with the same discretion as all Fortis instructions. Clients may share reports with their professional advisors without restriction.

Report structure

Every Fortis security review report follows a consistent structure: an executive summary suitable for professional third parties, a threat and vulnerability assessment referenced to the client's specific circumstances, findings presented by priority and risk level, costed and practical recommendations, and a closing advisory note on implementation and review cadence. The report is not a catalogue of problems. It is a document that supports decision-making.

Who benefits from a security review

Not only those who feel
at immediate risk.

Many of the clients we work with do not come to us because they are frightened. They come because they are thoughtful. They have assets, responsibilities, or a public profile that creates exposure they want to understand properly, and they want an independent professional view rather than a sales conversation.

A security review is appropriate whenever a client is making a significant decision about a property, changing their living or working arrangements, becoming more prominent professionally or publicly, or simply wants to understand their current security position with more clarity than they currently have.

It is also a valuable document for insurance purposes. Many high-value residential insurers require or give significant weight to an independent security assessment as part of their underwriting process.

  • —
    Before purchasing a significant property

    An independent security assessment of a property before purchase provides an objective view of its current security position, what investment is required, and what the ongoing risk profile looks like.

  • —
    Following a change in circumstances

    Promotion, a public appointment, a business transaction, or a change in personal relationships can alter a client's risk profile significantly. A review establishes where that profile now sits.

  • —
    As part of an ongoing security programme

    For clients with a retained security structure, an independent annual or biennial review provides an objective audit of what is working, what has changed, and where gaps exist.
